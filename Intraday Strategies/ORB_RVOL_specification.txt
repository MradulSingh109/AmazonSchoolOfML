2.  Core  Market  Inefficiency  
The  strategy  exploits  the  tendency  of  financial  markets  to  underreact  to  overnight  information.  
Examples  include:  
●  Earnings  releases  ●  Corporate  announcements  ●  Regulatory  developments  ●  Global  market  movements  ●  Macroeconomic  news  ●  Sector-specific  events  
When  significant  information  arrives  outside  market  hours,  institutional  participants  frequently  
require
 
substantial
 
time
 
to
 
execute
 
large
 
orders
 
after
 
the
 
market
 
opens.
 
This  creates:  
●  Elevated  opening  volume  ●  Persistent  directional  flows  ●  Delayed  price  discovery  ●  Momentum  continuation  throughout  the  trading  session  
The  strategy  identifies  these  situations  through  abnormal  opening  volume  and  participates  only  
after
 
directional
 
confirmation.

6.  Opening  Range  Construction  
The  Opening  Range  (OR)  is  defined  using  the  first  five-minute  candle  of  the  trading  session.  
--- Page 5 ---
Time  Interval:  
09:15  →  09:20  
 
Opening  Range  High  
H_OR  =  High  of  first  5-minute  candle  
 
Opening  Range  Low  
L_OR  =  Low  of  first  5-minute  candle  
 
Opening  Range  Width  
ORW  =  H_OR  −  L_OR  
 
Opening  Range  Percentage  
ORW%  =  ((H_OR  −  L_OR)  /  Previous  Day  Close)  ×  100  
 
7.  Relative  Volume  (RVOL)  
Objective  
Measure  whether  current  opening  activity  is  significantly  greater  than  normal  participation.  
 
Opening  Volume  
--- Page 6 ---
V_today  =  Volume  of  today's  first  5-minute  candle  
 
Historical  Baseline  
Average  volume  of  the  first  five-minute  candle  over  the  previous  14  trading  sessions.  
AvgVol14  =  Mean(V1,  V2,  ...  V14)  
 
RVOL  Calculation  
RVOL  =  V_today  /  AvgVol14  
 
Qualification  Rule  
RVOL  ≥  3.0  
Only  stocks  meeting  this  threshold  become  eligible  for  trading.

13.  Risk  Unit  (R)  
Define:  
R  =  |Entry  −  Stop|  
This  represents  the  initial  trade  risk.  
 
Example  
Entry  =  ₹500  
Stop  =  ₹490  
R  =  ₹10

14.  Profit  Management  
Initial  Objective  
Target  =  3R  
Long:  
Target  =  Entry  +  (3  ×  R)  
Short:  
Target  =  Entry  −  (3  ×  R)  
 
Dynamic  Trailing  System  
At  1.5R  Profit  
Move  stop  to  breakeven.  
 
At  2R  Profit  
Trail  stop  using  1  ATR.  
 
Beyond  2R  
Continue  ATR  trailing  until  exit.  
 
15.  Failed  Breakout  Exit  
Breakouts  occasionally  fail  and  re-enter  the  opening  range.  
To  identify  failed  breakouts:  
--- Page 12 ---
If  the  stock  closes  back  inside  the  opening  range  for  three  consecutive  bars:  
Exit  immediately.  
 
Long  Trade  Failure  
L_OR  <  Close  <  H_OR  
for  3  consecutive  bars.  
 
Short  Trade  Failure  
L_OR  <  Close  <  H_OR  
for  3  consecutive  bars.

The  following  filters  can  materially  improve  robustness:  
VWAP  Confirmation

Shorts  only  below  VWAP.

Trade  only  stocks  with:  
Absolute  Gap  ≥  1%  
 
Stronger  RVOL  Threshold  
Increase  threshold:  
RVOL  ≥  4

Shorts  only  when  NIFTY  50  <  20  EMA.  
 
Candidate  Ranking  
Rank  eligible  stocks  by:  
Score  =  RVOL  ×  Gap%  
Trade  only  the  highest-ranked  opportunities  each  session.
